How to Make 20000mAh 40W Router UPS
& High Power Bank

Stop worrying about load shedding. Build a professional-grade backup system that keeps your WiFi router running and charges your devices on the go.

Project Overview

This DIY project allows you to create a compact, efficient, and budget-friendly UPS system. By combining high-capacity Li-Po batteries with a smart boost/buck system, you can ensure your internet never drops during power failures.

Router Backup Stable voltage output ensures your router stays online without restarting.
Portable Power Bank Integrated USB outputs to charge smartphones and tablets.

3 Assembly Guide

01.

Battery & BMS Connection

Connect the two 10,000mAh Li-Po cells in parallel to achieve a total capacity of 20,000mAh. Connect these to the BMS (Battery Management System) to ensure safe charging and discharging.

02.

Voltage Boosting

Connect the battery output to the Boost Modules. Adjust the potentiometers on the modules to your required voltage (usually 9V or 12V depending on your router).

03.

Adding the Power Bank Function

Connect the Buck Converter and USB output module in parallel with the main power line. This allows you to use the system as a portable charger for your mobile devices.

04.

Final Assembly

Install the power switch for easy control and house everything inside a compact enclosure for a professional finish.
